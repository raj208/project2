Santhal Religion

Santhal religion is deeply rooted in animism. The Santhal believe in the presence of spirits in natural elements such as trees, mountains,rivers, and animals. These spirits are considered powerful and are integral to their cosmology.

The Santhals have a strong tradition of ancestor worship. They believe in maintaining a connection with their ancestors and seek their blessings for the well-being and prosperity of the community. Santhals worship a pantheon of deities known as Bongas. Each Bonga is associated with specific aspects of nature and is revered to ensure the harmony between humans and the environment. Santhals celebrate various rituals and festivals that are closely linked to their agrarian lifestyle. Festivals like Sohrai and Baha are significant and involve elaborate rituals, dances, and music. These celebrations often mark important agricultural milestones such as sowing and harvesting.

Santhal Culture

Language:

The Santhali language is central to Santhal culture. It is a Santhali language and is spoken by the Santhal community. The language has its own script, Ol Chiki, which is used for writing in Santhali.

Dance and Music:

Santhali dance and music are vibrant and play a crucial role in their cultural expression. Traditional dances like the "Chadar Badar" and "Langre" are performed during festivals and celebrations. The music involves the use of traditional instruments like the "Banam" (a string instrument) and "Tamak" (a percussion instrument).

Art and Craft:

Santhals are skilled in various forms of art and craft. They are known for creating intricate traditional jewelry, particularly for women. Santhal art often reflects their close connection with nature and their animistic beliefs.

Traditional Clothing:

 The traditional attire of Santhal men includes dhoti,kurta, and a headgear, while women wear sarees with a distinct pattern. The clothing often features vibrant colors and intricate embroidery.

Social Structure:

 Santhal society has a well-defined social structure. Theyhave a council of elders known as the "Manjhi" or "Majhi," who play a significant role in decision-making and dispute resolution. The community is organized into clans, and social cohesion is maintained through communal activities and rituals.

Housing:

 Traditional Santhal houses are made of materials like mud, wood, and thatch. The houses are designed to suit the agrarian lifestyle and are often surrounded by fields.
